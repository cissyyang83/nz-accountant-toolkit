  • FDR annual method: 5% of opening market value plus quick sale adjustment.
  • FDR quick sale adjustment: the lesser of peak holding method amount and actual gain.
  • Peak holding method amount: 5% x peak holding differential x average cost. Peak holding differential is the lesser of peak minus opening shares and peak minus closing shares.
  • CV method: closing market value plus gains, less opening market value plus costs. This tool includes disposal proceeds, dividends/holding gains and foreign tax credits as gains, and purchase costs plus other CV costs as costs.
  • Where FDR/CV choice is available, CV is floored at zero in this tool. Review non-ordinary share and other special cases separately.
  • Cost method: 5% of cost-method opening value plus the peak holding method amount.
  • This tool follows Inland Revenue's main calculator methods only. It does not decide exemptions, method eligibility, disclosure requirements, tax credits, DRR, RAM, or attributable FIF income.
